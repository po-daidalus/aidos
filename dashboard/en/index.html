English overview

How many businesses had Google reviews removed for defamation?

Since the EU Digital Services Act, Google Maps displays a public transparency notice on business profiles stating how many reviews were removed in the past 365 days following defamation complaints. aidos systematically collects and evaluates these public notices for Germany — neutral, source-linked, and updated monthly.

What we publish

  • A ranked, searchable dataset of affected businesses and chains across 16 major German cities — currently around 950 profiles that carry the removal notice.
  • Measured prevalence: we also count the profiles without a notice. Across the cities surveyed so far, only roughly 3–5 % of checked profiles carry it.
  • Estimates, clearly labelled: for sufficiently documented profiles we model where the rating would stand if the removed reviews still counted — always as a range, never as false precision.

What the data can and cannot say

A high number of removed reviews is not evidence of wrongdoing — businesses are frequently targeted by unjustified fake-review campaigns, and removals can be entirely legitimate. Google does not disclose who requested a removal or when individual reviews were removed. We publish company names only for legal entities and chains, never for private individuals.

Data access & press

The full interactive dataset, methodology and monthly reports are currently available in German. Citation format: “aidos.tech, data as of [month year]”. CSV extracts for journalistic use are available on request.
