Historical development

Blue = rating trajectory of today's visible reviews · bars = monthly review volume · red = estimated level with the removed reviews. The blue line is reconstructed from the reviews visible today (102 dated) — the removed ones were never part of it, so it shows no removal jump; it ends at today's displayed rating. The red corridor shows where the rating would stand today if the removed reviews (assumed 1–2★) still counted — its width reflects the double uncertainty (Google publishes only a range, the stars are assumed). Deliberately flat: a present-day reference level, not a historical measurement.

Red background: the past 365 days for which Google reports the number of removed reviews. Before the removal, the actually displayed rating was probably closer to the red corridor and rose to the blue line's level with the removal — when, Google does not publish, which is why we show the effect as a present-day level, not a curve. Values are snapshots of the public Google data.

What would the rating be without the removed reviews?

Assuming the removed reviews would have averaged 1–2★, the rating would arithmetically stand at ~4.7★ instead of the displayed 4.8★ — the removals lift the display by an estimated +0.1★. An estimate only, no exact values — if the removals were justified (e.g. fake campaigns), the displayed rating is the more accurate one.
